Read First — Eclipse Aftermath

A total solar eclipse crosses Iceland on 12 August 2026 — the day before you land. Your 13 August arrival falls squarely in the busiest, most expensive window of the year, with post-eclipse crowds still leaving the country. The trip absolutely works, but treat booking as urgent: reserve the 7-seat 4WD and every hotel now, above all the first four nights (Reykjavík, Hella, Vík). Here, availability — not budget — is the real constraint.

Trip at a Glance

How the trip is built

Built around the family

  • Comfortable pace. Driving days capped at about 3 hours wherever possible, with hotel arrival by 5pm. Two deliberate exceptions: Day 2 (volcano + Golden Circle) and Day 10 (the cross-country drive).
  • Hotels every night. Two rooms per night — a double for the parents, a triple or family room for the brothers.
  • One marquee experience per day. Glacier hike, ice cave, whale watching, hot springs — never stacked.
  • Walking under an hour at most stops. Exceptions where the destination earns it (Inside the Volcano on Day 2, optional Svartifoss on Day 5).
  • Two-night stays in Vík and Mývatn so there's no constant packing and unpacking.
  • One big adventure day — a guided Super Jeep expedition into the Askja highlands (Day 8), the trip's wild card.
  • Clockwise by default — starts and ends in Reykjavík, front-loading the famous south-coast sights. Prefer to dodge the post-eclipse crowds? See the reverse loop tab below.

Same ring, two directions. Clockwise hits the iconic south coast first; the reverse loop heads northwest first — built to dodge the post-eclipse crowds and ease the south-coast bed crunch. Dates, experiences and budget are identical; only the order changes.

Day 02Fri · 14 Aug
MarqueeLong Day

Inside the Volcano + Golden Circle

Reykjavík → Golden Circle → Hella · ~200 km
🌋
Today's Highlight
Descend 120 metres into a dormant magma chamber the size of a cathedral — the only place on Earth this is possible — then loop the Golden Circle on the way out of the city.
⏱️
The trip's one big day
Dropping the second Reykjavík night means pairing the volcano with the Golden Circle today. August daylight runs to ~10pm, so there's time — but expect a full day and a later check-in. If it's too much, skip Þingvellir and do the Golden Circle as a half-loop.

The day's plan

  • Þríhnúkagígur (Inside the Volcano) tour — bus pickup from Reykjavík at 8am, ~6 hours total, back by ~2:30pm
  • Lava-field hike (~45 min each way, easy) and the open lift 120 m down; lamb soup at base camp included
  • From ~3pm, drive the Golden Circle eastward toward Hella:
  • Þingvellir National Park — the rift between two continental plates, 30 min walk
  • Geysir geothermal area — Strokkur erupts every 6–8 minutes, no walking needed
  • Gullfoss waterfall — two-tier waterfall, 5 min walk from the car park
  • Secret Lagoon (Flúðir) — evening soak (open till ~10pm), far quieter than the Blue Lagoon
  • Short final drive to Hella for the night
Stay: Hella / Hvolsvöllur Soak: Secret Lagoon Book: Volcano 4+ months ahead

Day 08Thu · 20 Aug
MarqueeHighland

Askja Highland Super Jeep

Mývatn (base) · guided F-road expedition
🚙
Today's Highlight
The trip's wildest day — a guided Super Jeep across the highland interior to the Askja caldera, fording rivers and crossing the lava desert NASA once trained the Apollo astronauts on. A pro drives, so the F roads and the river crossing are someone else's job.
⚠️
Know before you book
A long, remote 10–11 hour day on rough roads — the most demanding day of the trip. Going guided keeps your rental insurance valid (self-driving F roads voids it). Víti crater bathing is sometimes closed for safety — treat a swim as a bonus, not a given.

The day's plan

  • Early pickup from your Mývatn hotel — operators running this from Reykjahlíð include Geo Travel and Saga Travel (confirm the day-tour and pickup point when booking)
  • Super Jeep south across the Ódáðahraun lava desert, with one or more river crossings (F88 / F894)
  • Walk to Askja and Öskjuvatn — a vast flooded caldera ringed by mountains (~45 min each way)
  • Víti crater — milky geothermal lake at the bottom; a soak if it's open that day
  • Drekagil canyon at the Dreki huts, and on many tours the fresh 2014 Holuhraun lava field
  • Return to Mývatn in the evening — a soak at the Nature Baths is the perfect wind-down
Stay: Mývatn / Reykjahlíð (2 of 2) Guided: Super Jeep Book: 1–2 months ahead

Reverse Loop

The 12-day plan, counter-clockwise

Why drive it in reverse?

  • Dodge the eclipse crowds. Clockwise puts you on the south coast — Reykjavík, the Golden Circle, Vík, Jökulsárlón — during 13–16 Aug, the peak post-eclipse jam. Reverse heads northwest first and saves those honeypots for 21–24 Aug, once most eclipse visitors have flown home.
  • Easier beds. The scarce early-trip nights become Reykjavík, Snæfellsnes and Akureyri — more capacity (Akureyri is a city) — and you reach Vík and Höfn later, when cancellations have freed up rooms.
  • The tradeoff. The 480 km cross-country drive lands on Day 4 instead of Day 10, and you save the famous south coast for last. If you'd rather have instant wow and the simplest first-night logistics, stay clockwise.

Practical Notes

Things to know and do

Book These First

  • 7-seat 4WD SUV — supply is tight, book now
  • Inside the Volcano — single operator, May–Oct only, sells out months ahead
  • Hotels in small towns — Hella, Vík, Höfn, Mývatn, Snæfellsnes fill 4–6 months out (sooner given the eclipse)
  • Askja Super Jeep day (Day 8) — Geo Travel or Saga Travel, runs summer only, book 1–2 months ahead
  • Húsavík whale watching — book 2–3 months ahead for August morning slots
  • Katla Ice Cave — Troll Expeditions or Katlatrack from Vík
  • Jökulsárlón boat — slots fill on busy days, book 1–2 weeks ahead

What August in Iceland is Like

  • Daylight — about 16 hours at the start, 14 by the end. No real night.
  • Weather — 8 to 14°C, often windy, frequent showers
  • Pack — layers, real waterproof jacket, decent walking shoes
  • Puffins — mostly gone by late August, treat sightings as a bonus
  • Aurora — too bright in August, don't plan around it
  • Eclipse aftermath — you arrive 13 Aug, the day after the 12 Aug total eclipse. The first ~4 nights are the busiest, priciest of the year — book those immediately and expect crowds at south-coast sights

Driving in Iceland

  • International Driving Permit needed — get from AA Singapore
  • Drive on the right
  • Ring Road and Snæfellsnes are fully paved — no rough roads on your own driving
  • Askja (Day 8) is the one exception — rough F-roads and a river crossing, but you go in a guided Super Jeep, so the operator handles it and your rental insurance stays valid (self-driving F-roads voids it)
  • New 2026 km tax replaces fuel tax (~ISK 7/km), built into rental settlement
  • Speed limits enforced — 90 km/h paved rural, 50 in towns
  • Fuel stations sparse in the east — fill up when half-empty
  • Watch for sheep on the road — slow down on rural stretches
  • Check safetravel.is every morning before driving

If Things Go Wrong

  • Volcano tour cancelled (high winds) — Raufarhólshellir lava tunnel is the backup
  • Day 10 long drive too much — split it with a night in Sauðárkrókur
  • Snæfellsnes loop too tiring — cut to half (south coast or north only)
  • Jökulsárlón boat sold out — Diamond Beach across the road is free and still spectacular
  • Askja day cancelled (weather or impassable rivers — possible even in August) — operators reschedule or refund; fall back to a relaxed Mývatn day (Nature Baths, Dimmuborgir, Hverir)
  • Mývatn weather bad — the Nature Baths alone justify the night
  • Whale tour weather cancellation — most operators offer free reschedule next day
