Read First — Eclipse Aftermath

A total solar eclipse crosses Iceland on 12 August 2026 — the day before you land. Your 13 August arrival falls squarely in the busiest, most expensive window of the year, with post-eclipse crowds still leaving the country. The trip absolutely works, but treat booking as urgent: reserve the 7-seat 4WD and every hotel now, above all the first four nights (Reykjavík, Hella, Vík). Here, availability — not budget — is the real constraint.

Trip at a Glance

How the trip is built

Built around the family

  • Comfortable pace. Driving days capped at about 3 hours wherever possible, with hotel arrival by 5pm. Two deliberate exceptions: Day 2 (volcano + Golden Circle) and Day 10 (the cross-country drive).
  • Hotels every night. Two rooms per night — a double for the parents, a triple or family room for the brothers.
  • One marquee experience per day. Glacier hike, ice cave, whale watching, hot springs — never stacked.
  • Walking under an hour at most stops. Exceptions where the destination earns it (Inside the Volcano on Day 2, optional Svartifoss on Day 5).
  • Two-night stays in Vík and Akureyri so there's no constant packing and unpacking.
  • One full rest day in Akureyri (Day 9) — no driving, no agenda.
  • Clockwise loop starting and ending in Reykjavík. Front-loads the famous south-coast sights so the energy is highest when crowds are biggest.

Day 01Thu · 13 Aug
Arrival

Arrive in Reykjavík

Keflavík Airport → Reykjavík · 50 km · ~50 min
✈️
Today's Highlight
Land at 3:15pm, collect the car, and ease in. One Reykjavík night only — keep tonight gentle and save your energy for the volcano tomorrow.

The day's plan

  • Land 3:15pm — clear immigration and pick up the rental car at Keflavík Airport (allow ~1 hour, longer in eclipse-week crowds)
  • Drive to Reykjavík (~50 min) and check into the hotel
  • Gentle evening stroll — Sun Voyager by the water and Harpa on the harbour, then up Laugavegur
  • Photo stop at Hallgrímskirkja — the tower can wait for the last day
  • Optional, if you have the energy: a soak at Sky Lagoon (open late, infinity edge over the Atlantic)

Day 02Fri · 14 Aug
MarqueeLong Day

Inside the Volcano + Golden Circle

Reykjavík → Golden Circle → Hella · ~200 km
🌋
Today's Highlight
Descend 120 metres into a dormant magma chamber the size of a cathedral — the only place on Earth this is possible — then loop the Golden Circle on the way out of the city.
⏱️
The trip's one big day
Dropping the second Reykjavík night means pairing the volcano with the Golden Circle today. August daylight runs to ~10pm, so there's time — but expect a full day and a later check-in. If it's too much, skip Þingvellir and do the Golden Circle as a half-loop.

The day's plan

  • Þríhnúkagígur (Inside the Volcano) tour — bus pickup from Reykjavík at 8am, ~6 hours total, back by ~2:30pm
  • Lava-field hike (~45 min each way, easy) and the open lift 120 m down; lamb soup at base camp included
  • From ~3pm, drive the Golden Circle eastward toward Hella:
  • Þingvellir National Park — the rift between two continental plates, 30 min walk
  • Geysir geothermal area — Strokkur erupts every 6–8 minutes, no walking needed
  • Gullfoss waterfall — two-tier waterfall, 5 min walk from the car park
  • Secret Lagoon (Flúðir) — evening soak (open till ~10pm), far quieter than the Blue Lagoon
  • Short final drive to Hella for the night
Stay: Hella / Hvolsvöllur Soak: Secret Lagoon Book: Volcano 4+ months ahead
Day 03Sat · 15 Aug
DriveNature

South Coast — Waterfalls and Black Beaches

Hella → Vík · 130 km · all-day with stops
🌊
Today's Highlight
The most photographed stretch in Iceland — walk behind a waterfall, see a glacier, stand on black sand. Almost all of it visible from the car.

The day's plan

  • Seljalandsfoss — the waterfall you can walk behind. Bring a raincoat.
  • Gljúfrabúi — the hidden waterfall 200m further along, often missed
  • Skógafoss — wide, thundering waterfall, 5 min walk
  • Sólheimajökull glacier viewpoint — see a real glacier, 15 min walk
  • Dyrhólaey arch — sea cliffs and rock arch (puffins may have left by late August)
  • Reynisfjara black sand beach — basalt columns. Stay back from the surf — the waves are dangerous
  • Check into hotel in Vík
Stay: Vík (1 of 2) Drive: ~2 hours total Pack: Waterproofs
Day 04Sun · 16 Aug
Marquee

Katla Ice Cave

Vík (base) · Super Jeep ice cave tour
🧊
Today's Highlight
Iceland's only natural ice cave open year-round. Black volcanic ash streaked through 800-year-old blue ice. Easy walk after the jeep ride.

The day's plan

  • Morning Katla Ice Cave tour from Vík (~3 hours total) — alternative operator: Katlatrack
  • Super Jeep across black sand plains for 40 minutes
  • 20-minute walk on flat ash to the cave mouth
  • Crampons on for the cave exploration
  • Return to Vík for lunch
  • Free afternoon — second visit to Reynisfjara, or rest at the hotel
  • Optional: Lava Show Vík in the evening (45 min, real molten lava)
Stay: Vík (2 of 2) Walking: 40 min total Book: 3+ months ahead

Glaciers and the Iceberg Lagoon

Vík → Höfn · 270 km · ~4.5 hours with stops
🏔️
Today's Highlight
A long driving day, but the prize is unforgettable — boats weaving between icebergs the size of houses in the glacier lagoon.

The day's plan

  • Eldhraun lava field — 800 km² of moss-covered lava, drive-through

  • Jökulsárlón glacier lagoon — amphibian boat tour, 45 min, no walking
  • Diamond Beach — icebergs washed up on black sand, across the road
  • Final hour of driving to Höfn
  • Dinner at Pakkhús or Humarhöfnin — try the langoustine, Höfn's specialty

Day 06Tue · 18 Aug
Scenic Drive

The East Fjords

Höfn → Egilsstaðir · 250 km · ~4 hours
🏞️
Today's Highlight
Iceland's least-touristed coastline. The drive itself is the experience — winding fjord roads, fishing villages, mountains rising sheer from the sea.

The day's plan

  • Djúpivogur — coffee stop, see the egg sculptures on the harbour
  • Petra's Stone Collection in Stöðvarfjörður — an old woman's lifetime of Icelandic geology
  • Stop wherever the view looks good — there are no wrong stops
  • Optional detour: Seyðisfjörður — the famous rainbow road and blue church (25 min off route)
  • Vök Baths — geothermal pools floating on a lake, just outside Egilsstaðir
  • Check into hotel in Egilsstaðir
Stay: Egilsstaðir Drive: 4 hours (scenic) Pace: Relaxed

Volcanic North & Mývatn

Egilsstaðir → Mývatn · 165 km · ~3 hours with stops
♨️
Today's Highlight
Sulphur fields, lava castles, bubbling mud — then a soak at the northern equivalent of the Blue Lagoon with a fraction of the crowds.

The day's plan

  • Drive across the highland plateau toward Mývatn
  • Námaskarð (Hverir) — steaming sulphur fields and bubbling mud, 10–15 min loop
  • Lunch in Reykjahlíð
  • Dimmuborgir — twisted lava formations, choose a 15 min or 1 hour loop
  • Grjótagjá — small lava cave with a blue thermal pool (look only, too hot to bathe)
  • Mývatn Nature Baths — late afternoon soak, plan 90 minutes
Stay: Mývatn / Reykjahlíð Drive: 3 hours Soak: Mývatn Baths

The day's plan

Practical Notes

Things to know and do

Book These First

  • 7-seat 4WD SUV — supply is tight, book now
  • Inside the Volcano — single operator, May–Oct only, sells out months ahead
  • Hotels in small towns — Hella, Vík, Höfn, Mývatn, Snæfellsnes fill 4–6 months out (sooner given the eclipse)
  • Húsavík whale watching — book 2–3 months ahead for August morning slots
  • Katla Ice Cave — Troll Expeditions or Katlatrack from Vík
  • Jökulsárlón boat — slots fill on busy days, book 1–2 weeks ahead

What August in Iceland is Like

  • Daylight — about 16 hours at the start, 14 by the end. No real night.
  • Weather — 8 to 14°C, often windy, frequent showers
  • Pack — layers, real waterproof jacket, decent walking shoes
  • Puffins — mostly gone by late August, treat sightings as a bonus
  • Aurora — too bright in August, don't plan around it
  • Eclipse aftermath — you arrive 13 Aug, the day after the 12 Aug total eclipse. The first ~4 nights are the busiest, priciest of the year — book those immediately and expect crowds at south-coast sights

Driving in Iceland

  • International Driving Permit needed — get from AA Singapore
  • Drive on the right
  • Ring Road and Snæfellsnes are fully paved — no rough roads on this route
  • New 2026 km tax replaces fuel tax (~ISK 7/km), built into rental settlement
  • Speed limits enforced — 90 km/h paved rural, 50 in towns
  • Fuel stations sparse in the east — fill up when half-empty
  • Watch for sheep on the road — slow down on rural stretches
  • Check safetravel.is every morning before driving

If Things Go Wrong

  • Volcano tour cancelled (high winds) — Raufarhólshellir lava tunnel is the backup
  • Day 10 long drive too much — split it with a night in Sauðárkrókur
  • Snæfellsnes loop too tiring — cut to half (south coast or north only)
  • Jökulsárlón boat sold out — Diamond Beach across the road is free and still spectacular
  • Mývatn weather bad — the Nature Baths alone justify the night
  • Whale tour weather cancellation — most operators offer free reschedule next day
